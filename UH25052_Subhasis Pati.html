About the System

What is 360-Degree Feedback?

360-degree feedback is a comprehensive performance evaluation method that collects structured input from multiple stakeholders in an employee's work environment — not just their direct manager.

Multi-Source Intelligence
Feedback is gathered from managers, peers, direct reports, and the employee themselves — creating a 360-degree view of performance and behaviour.
Bias Reduction
By aggregating multiple perspectives, the system neutralises single-rater bias and personal prejudice that is common in traditional appraisals.
Development Focus
Rather than purely evaluative, CorpSync 360 is oriented toward growth — generating Individual Development Plans (IDPs) directly from feedback data.
Self-Awareness
Comparing self-ratings with others' ratings surfaces blind spots and helps employees understand how they are perceived in the workplace.

Methodology

Weighted Scoring Model

A transparent, configurable weighting algorithm ensures the final score reflects the organisational hierarchy and strategic priorities.

Manager40%
Peers20%
Direct Reports20%
Self20%
Weighted Score Formula
WS = (Mgr × 0.40) + (Peer × 0.20)
      + (Report × 0.20) + (Self × 0.20)

Example (Priya Sharma):
WS = (4.3 × 0.40) + (4.2 × 0.20)
      + (4.0 × 0.20) + (3.8 × 0.20)
WS = 1.72 + 0.84 + 0.80 + 0.76
WS = 4.12 → rounded to 4.1

Benefits

Advantages of 360° Feedback

Why leading organisations choose multi-source feedback over traditional single-manager evaluations.

🌐
Holistic Performance View

Captures performance from multiple angles — strategic, collaborative, and interpersonal — that no single evaluator can fully observe.

⚖️
Reduced Single-Rater Bias

Aggregating multiple perspectives neutralises personal bias, favouritism, and the "halo effect" common in traditional appraisals.

🌱
Employee Development Focus

Feedback is specific, behavioural, and actionable — directly feeding into Individual Development Plans that drive real growth.

🔍
Enhanced Self-Awareness

Comparing self-ratings with external ratings surfaces blind spots and motivates genuine reflection and change in behaviour.

💬
Improved Communication

The process itself encourages honest dialogue, psychological safety, and a feedback culture throughout the organisation.

📊
Data-Driven HR Decisions

Quantified competency scores provide objective evidence for succession planning, promotion decisions, and L&D investment.

Limitations

Challenges & Risks

Implementing 360° feedback responsibly requires HR leaders to proactively address these known challenges.

⚠️
Risk of Dishonest Ratings

Personal relationships may cause raters to inflate or deflate scores strategically rather than honestly reflecting performance.

👥
Peer Bias & Group Dynamics

Social proximity, competition, or clique effects can skew peer ratings, especially in close-knit or politically charged teams.

🔒
Confidentiality Concerns

Despite anonymisation, employees may fear that specific feedback could be traced back to them in small teams or niche roles.

😟
Fear of Retaliation

Subordinates and peers may withhold honest feedback about managers due to fear of negative consequences — undermining data quality.

📋
Process Over-Complexity

Poorly designed forms or long cycles lead to rater fatigue, low completion rates, and superficial responses that reduce validity.

🚫
Misuse for Punishment

When used punitively rather than developmentally, 360° feedback generates resentment and destroys psychological safety in teams.

Best Practices

System Recommendations

Evidence-based design principles for implementing 360° feedback that is fair, trusted, and genuinely developmental.

🛡️
Guarantee Anonymity

Ensure minimum rater thresholds (3+ per category) before releasing data. Never reveal individual responses to managers or HR.

🎓
Train All Raters First

Rater calibration workshops ensure consistent understanding of the rating scale and reduce halo, leniency, and recency biases.

💡
Prioritise Development

Frame the entire process as a growth tool, not an evaluation instrument. Development plans should be the primary output.

👁️
Ensure HR Oversight

HRBP should review all reports before release to flag statistical outliers, moderate extreme scores, and ensure quality.

🤝
Provide Post-Feedback Coaching

Every employee receiving a report should have a structured conversation with their manager and access to a professional coach.

📅
Run Cycles Periodically

Annual cycles provide benchmark data. Mid-year pulse checks maintain momentum and allow timely course corrections.

📝
Combine Quant + Qual Feedback

Likert scores alone are insufficient. Open-ended qualitative comments provide the context and specificity needed for real change.

📄

Export Feedback Reports

Download individual or consolidated feedback summaries as formatted PDF reports for archival, coaching sessions, or HR audit purposes.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about how CorpSync 360 works and how your data is handled.

Yes. All peer and subordinate responses are fully anonymised. Individual responses are never shared with managers or the reviewee. Only aggregated data (minimum 3 raters per category) is displayed in reports. The system uses end-to-end encryption and does not log rater identity with responses.
The final report is accessible only to the reviewee, their HR Business Partner (HRBP), and the direct line manager. Senior leadership can access anonymised, aggregated department-level data only. Peer and subordinate individual responses are never accessible to anyone.
The overall score is a weighted average: Manager (40%), Peers (20%), Direct Reports (20%), and Self (20%). Weights are configurable by HR administration to align with organisational priorities. Each competency is rated on a 1–5 scale and averaged within each rater group before weighting is applied.
CorpSync 360 is designed primarily as a development tool. While 360° data may inform talent calibration discussions, it is not intended to be used as the sole or primary basis for promotion or compensation decisions. Its core purpose is to generate actionable Individual Development Plans.
Best practice recommends an annual full cycle supplemented by a mid-year pulse check. For high-potential (HiPo) employees or those in leadership development programmes, bi-annual cycles may be appropriate. Running cycles too frequently can cause rater fatigue and reduce response quality.
All reports are reviewed by your HRBP before release. If significant developmental feedback is identified, a coaching session will be arranged. Feedback is framed constructively and the system's guidelines discourage punitive language. You will always have support through the IDP process.
Employees may nominate peers they would like to include, but the final rater list is confirmed by your direct manager and HRBP to ensure balance, representativeness, and avoidance of strategic selection bias. A minimum of 3 peers and 2 direct reports (where applicable) is required.
